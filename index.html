Engineering Immunotherapies for Solid Tumors — CAR T Cell Research at UCSF

About Me

Hello! My name is Safwaan Khan. I am a current Ph.D. student in the Pharmaceutical Sciences and Pharmacogenomics (PSPG) program at UCSF.

As an undergraduate student at UCLA, under the direction of Dr. Owen Witte, I contributed to novel antigen discovery for the development of immunotherapies for solid tumors (CRPC, NEPC, SCLC). In my graduate studies, under the direction of Dr. Hideho Okada and Dr. Justin Eyquem, I hope to utilize my interests in cancer biology, neuroimmunology, and bioengineering to design and optimize immunotherapies for solid tumors, specifically in the context of brain cancer.

Research Experience

  • Eyquem Lab, UCSF (2025–): Engineered CAR-T therapies against solid tumor antigens. Designed and cloned AAV vectors.
  • Okada Lab, UCSF (2024): Studied hypoxic glioblastoma regions; identified splice epitope upregulation via bioinformatics.
  • Owen Witte Lab, UCLA (2022–2024): Developed TCRs for PAP. Isolated MHC complexes for MS. Engineered nanobody-antibody constructs.
  • Loes Loohuis Lab, UCLA (2021–2023): Helped build Latin American biobank; coordinated neurobehavioral data collection.
